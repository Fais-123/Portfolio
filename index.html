Case Study: Employee Salary Analysis

Deep dive into data-driven HR insights

Problem Statement

A mid-sized company wanted to analyze salary distribution across departments to ensure fair pay, identify potential gaps, and design a data-driven compensation strategy. The challenge was to process large employee datasets and provide actionable insights for HR decision-making.

Dataset & Approach

Data Overview

  • 5,000+ employee records
  • 15 variables including salary, department, job role, and experience
  • 3 years of historical data
  • Data collected from multiple HR sources

Methodology

  • Data cleaning (handling missing values, outliers, duplicates)
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) with visualizations
  • Statistical analysis & salary benchmarking
  • Correlation study between salary, experience, and department

Key Findings & Insights

The analysis uncovered several critical insights:

  • Employees in the IT and Finance departments earned on average 15% higher salaries compared to similar roles in HR and Operations.
  • A strong positive correlation was observed between experience and compensation, but salary growth plateaued after 12 years of service.
  • Gender-based pay disparity was minimal overall, but certain departments showed a 5–7% gap that required HR review.
  • Several high-performing employees were identified as underpaid compared to industry benchmarks, signaling retention risks.

These insights guided HR to restructure salary bands, leading to a more equitable compensation strategy. The company implemented adjustments that improved employee satisfaction and reduced turnover risk.
